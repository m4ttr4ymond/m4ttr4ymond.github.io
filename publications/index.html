    Multi-modal generative models typically require abundant training data from multi-modal joint distributions, which is often unavailable in the life sciences. We propose to treat each modality as a marginal distribution and correct their independent diffusion processes. Specifically, we introduce "joint diffusion sampling," a method that generates samples from joint distributions using pre-trained models for individual (single-modal) marginal distributions and minimal data from the (multi-modal) joint distribution. In this work-in-progress submission, we demonstrate preliminary results on datasets with a single modality, including images and molecules, and discuss multi-modal applications of our approach.
